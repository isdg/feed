# [$] Topics in filesystem testing

It should come as no surprise that a gathering of filesystem developers would discuss filesystem testing; it has been a mainstay of the [Linux Storage,
Filesystem, Memory Management, and BPF Summit](https://events.linuxfoundation.org/lsfmmbpf/) over the years and the 2026 summit was no exception. Ted Ts'o led the discussion this time; he had a few different topics to raise, including his perception of increasing regressions for ext4 in the stable kernels and what can be done to help reduce them. As [with](https://lwn.net/Articles/789225/) [other](https://lwn.net/Articles/896523/) [similar](https://lwn.net/Articles/937830/) [sessions](https://lwn.net/Articles/982099/) at the summit over the years, there is a lot of interest in collaborating on test inputs and outputs, but finding a way to centralize that information has so far eluded the filesystem community.
