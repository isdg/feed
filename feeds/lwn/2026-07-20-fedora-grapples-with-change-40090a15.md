# [$] Fedora grapples with change

The [Fedora Project](https://fedoraproject.org/) is known for, among other things, having a well-defined set of processes for just about everything. It has extensive [packaging
guidelines](https://docs.fedoraproject.org/en-US/packaging-guidelines/) that deal with the complexities of creating RPMs to install software, as well as processes for managing the [legal questions](https://docs.fedoraproject.org/en-US/legal/) that arise around shipping software. Fedora also has a well-defined [change
process](https://docs.fedoraproject.org/en-US/operations/changes_guide/) for dealing with [self-contained technical changes](https://docs.fedoraproject.org/en-US/operations/changes_policy/#_self_contained_changes) as well as [major
changes to the distribution](https://docs.fedoraproject.org/en-US/operations/changes_policy/#_system_wide_changes), and other issues as they arise. At the moment, though, the project seems to be experiencing a sort of midlife crisis as it re-examines several of its change processes at once to determine if they are still effective.
